Data Cleaning Process

Data cleaning is a crucial step in preparing your dataset for analysis. It involves several key processes:

  • Handling Missing Values: Identify and handle missing data by either removing records with missing values or imputing them with appropriate values (e.g., mean, median, or mode).
  • Removing Duplicates: Check for and remove duplicate records to ensure that each entry in the dataset is unique.
  • Correcting Data Types: Ensure that each column in the dataset has the correct data type (e.g., integers, floats, dates) to facilitate accurate analysis.
  • Standardizing Formats: Standardize formats for categorical variables (e.g., converting all text to lowercase) and date formats to ensure consistency.
  • Outlier Detection: Identify and handle outliers that may skew the analysis, either by removing them or adjusting their values.

These steps help in enhancing the quality of the data, making it more suitable for analysis and modeling.
